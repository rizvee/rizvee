## 🗣️ Voice and Tone Guidelines

### What to Use
* **Business-Aware & Practical:** Focus heavily on *operational problems solved* and *business metrics impacted* (RTO reduction, manual work hours saved).
* **Confining & Direct:** Present clean, structured markdown panels. Use tables, lists, and code blocks rather than lengthy paragraphs.
* **Operator Mentality:** Show that you understand the reality of business constraints (shipping fees, inventory holding, conversion margins).
* **AI-First but Quality-Driven:** Emphasize strict linting, prompt structures, and automated remediation over blind copy-pasting.

### What to Avoid
* **Generic Buzzwords:** Avoid phrases like "10x Developer", "Rockstar Coder", "Passionate Tech Enthusiast", "Innovative Disruptor", or "AI Guru".
* **Unsupported Claims:** Do not make up metrics (e.g. "millions of users served") unless they are fully auditable and public.
* **Childish Visuals:** Avoid excessive emojis, erratic color combinations in banners, or low-contrast shields. Keep a cohesive TokyoNight dark blue and cyan accent theme.

## ⚙️ Maintenance & Update Protocol

### 1. Adding a New Repository
* **Classify it:** Determine if it is a major Featured System (requires placement in `README.md` and deep breakdown in `PROJECTS.md`) or a general public experiment (requires a clean row in the `PROJECTS.md` "Additional Public Repositories" table).
* **Verify Links:** Check that repository names match exactly (GitHub paths are case-sensitive).
* **Align Positioning:** Write the project description focusing first on the **Operational Problem Solved**, then the **Stack**, then the **Business Outcome**.

### 2. Branding Colors and Styling
Always maintain a cohesive, high-end technical aesthetic:
* **Background:** Deep Slate Dark (`#020617` to `#0f172a`)
* **Primary Accent Color:** Cyan (`#0ea5e9` to `#38bdf8`)
* **Badge Styling:** Use `style=for-the-badge` for main social links, and `style=for-the-badge&labelColor=020617` with custom icons for technical badges to maintain contrast.
